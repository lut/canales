CARLOS CANALES |

Carlos Canales is a Partner at Canales Auty who concentrates on project development, oil and gas projects, liquefied natural gas (LNG), power, nuclear and alternative energy transactions. Mr. Canales has extensive experience with oil and gas transactions, including assisting clients with the structuring of joint operating agreements (JOA), farm-outs, production sharing agreements (PSA), service contracts and unitization agreements for upstream developments in Mexico, Venezuela, Colombia, Uruguay, Peru and Ecuador. For the midstream and downstream sector, Mr. Canales has advised clients with the development and construction of regasification facility projects, LNG pooling agreements governing the sharing of storage, regasification send-out and nitrogen blending capacity among terminal users, LNG shipping matters and marine arrangements for LNG deliveries, natural gas transportation capacity agreements, EPC contracts for oil and gas pipelines, receiving terminals and oil products facilities, marketing and retail transactions.

Mr. Canales has also advised independent power producers and wind, solar and geothermal developers in EPC contracts, O&M agreements, interconnection, wheeling and energy management arrangements; nuclear power equipment and waste management service agreements. He has advised major international firms in numerous commercial transactions and bidding procedures before the Federal Electricity Commission (CFE).

Mr. Canales worked as Head of Legal and in-house Counsel for Shell Mexico, and Head of Legal for Iberdrola Ingenieria y Construcci√≥n; being responsible for providing legal services in numerous sensitive and highly complex enterprises in Mexico and Latin America.

In addition to his professional experience, Mr. Canales is a PhD Candidate in Public International Law, at the University of Leicester, whose research is focused on Joint Development and International Unitization Agreements, and holds an MBA in Oil & Gas Management and an LLM in Resources Law & Policy both from the Centre for Energy, Petroleum, Mineral Law and Policy, University of Dundee.
